## I. FOUNDING PRINCIPLE - The "Why"

### The Failure That Created Us

**What Failed:**
- **[[02 Player/Erukana (Nissen)/Factions/The Queensguard\|The Queensguard]]** fell to the purple plague because divine obedience made them rigid, unable to adapt
- **The Crown** allowed evil to fill the void - [[02 Player/Erukana (Nissen)/Factions/Ridderne af Blodrosen\|Blood Rose]] now occupies positions of power
- **The People** suffer under tyranny disguised as protection

**What We Know:**
- Blood Rose worships **[[02 Player/Erukana (Nissen)/Setting lore/Deities of Ceynor#Takhisis\|Takhisis]]** - goddess of tyranny, control, and chromatic dragons (Lawful Evil)
- They fight the purple plague not to save people, but to preserve their own control
- They impose order without consent - [[02 Player/Erukana (Nissen)/People/Baronesse Avalande\|Baronesse Avalande]] never agreed to their presence
- Evil wearing the mask of heroism is worse than naked evil

**Our Answer:**
> "When the Queensguard fell, tyranny filled the void. We will not repeat their mistakes—bound by rigid law and divine chains. But neither will we allow evil to wear the hero's mantle. The Watchers stand as predators hunting predators, protecting those who cannot protect themselves from ALL who would enslave them."

### Core Mission Statement

**Primary Enemies:**
1. **The Purple Plague** - Corruption that destroyed the Queensguard
2. **Blood Rose Order** - Servants of Takhisis imposing tyranny as "protection"
3. **All Oppressors** - Whether plague, goddess, or mortal tyrant

**Our Method:**
- Information defeats tyranny before it takes root
- Pragmatic action over dogmatic principle
- Freedom through strength, not divine mandate
- Hunt the hunters who prey upon the helpless

## II. ORGANIZATIONAL IDENTITY

### Name: **The Watchers of the Threshold**
**Common Name:** "The Watchers"

### Symbol
**Visual:** A wolf's eye within an open doorway
- **Wolf Eye:** Vigilant predator, natural law, never sleeping
- **Open Threshold:** Watching boundaries, but not imprisoning
- **Combined:** We guard freedom, not impose control

### The Watcher's Oath

*"I am eyes in darkness, voice for the silent.  
I hunt those who prey upon the helpless.  
I serve no master but truth and justice.  
I bow to no goddess of chains.  
I am Watcher. I am free."*

### Core Tenets

1. **Vigilance Without Tyranny**
   - We protect, but do not impose
   - We expose evil, but trust people to choose
   
2. **Information Is Our Weapon**
   - Knowledge defeats oppression before it spreads
   - Intelligence gathering is primary mission
   - Expose Blood Rose's true nature
   
3. **Natural Justice**
   - The strong protect the weak by natural right, not divine decree
   - We are predators who hunt predators
   - No chains of obedience—to god or mortal
   
4. **Pragmatic Morality**
   - We make hard choices the Queensguard couldn't
   - We adapt where rigid law fails
   - But we never serve tyranny to fight tyranny

5. **Pack Loyalty**
   - Horizontal structure, not hierarchy
   - Trust within, vigilance without
   - No member abandoned

## IV. ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE - Three Circles

### The Eyes (Outer Circle)
**Role:** Unwitting information sources  
**Awareness:** Don't know organization exists  
**Recruitment:** Tavern workers, merchants, travelers, informants  
**Function:** 
- Provide information for payment
- No oath, no commitment
- Can't betray what they don't know

**Example:** Barmaid who reports unusual visitors, merchant who notes troop movements

### The Watchers (Middle Circle)
**Role:** Active intelligence operatives  
**Awareness:** Know organization exists, have taken the oath  
**Recruitment:** Trusted individuals proven through action  
**Capabilities:**
- Active intelligence gathering
- Basic self-defense training
- Can operate independently
- Maintain cover identities

**Function:**
- Directed information collection
- Safe house maintenance  
- Support network for Hunters
- Recruit new Eyes

**Example:** Merchant with hidden allegiance, temple guard passing information, tavern owner managing safe house

**Role:** Leadership and direct action  
**Awareness:** Full operational knowledge  
**Recruitment:** Only proven Watchers or founding members  
**Capabilities:**
- Combat capable
- Strategic planning
- Lead missions
- Make organizational decisions

**Function:**
- Strategic direction
- High-risk operations
- Precision strikes when intelligence isn't enough
- Protect the organization itself

## VI. OPERATIONAL DOCTRINE

### Intelligence Gathering Priorities

**Tier 1: Immediate Threats**
- Blood Rose troop movements
- Purple plague outbreaks/agents  
- Blood Rose political maneuvering
- Threats to Baronesse Avalande

**Tier 2: Strategic Intelligence**
- Blood Rose command structure in Astley
- Takhisis worship patterns/rituals
- Purple plague origins/methods
- Allied organizations (who can be trusted)
- Enemy capabilities and weaknesses

**Tier 3: Opportunity Intelligence**
- Corruption in local government
- Merchants/nobles who might be recruited
- Safe routes for moving people/information
- Resources that could be acquired

### Rules of Engagement

**Information Warfare:**
1. Gather first, act second
2. Verify through multiple sources
3. Expose evil, don't become it
4. Truth is weapon against tyranny

**Direct Action:**
1. Only when intelligence isn't enough
2. Precision over confrontation
3. Protect innocents first
4. No member left behind

**Recruitment:**
1. Watch before approaching
2. Test loyalty through small tasks
3. Never reveal full organization to unproven
4. Those who serve tyranny cannot serve us

**Operational Security:**
1. Cells don't know other cells
2. True names only for Hunters
3. Multiple communication methods
4. Assume Blood Rose infiltration attempts

### Decision-Making Process

**Tactical Decisions** (field operations):
- Individual Watchers/Hunters make calls
- Report back to leadership
- Trust in training and judgment

**Strategic Decisions** (organizational direction):
- Hunters council meets
- Debate openly, decide collectively  
- Founder (Clarabel) has final say if consensus fails
- No divine mandate, just proven judgment

**Moral Dilemmas:**
- Hard choices will come
- We discuss openly, not hide from difficulty
- We learn from mistakes, don't repeat Queensguard's rigidity
- Some things are non-negotiable: no tyranny, no abandonment

## XIV. FOUNDING DOCUMENT

### The Compact

**We, the Founding Members of the Watchers of the Threshold, do hereby establish this organization with the following understanding:**

**Purpose:**
To stand vigilant against tyranny in all forms - whether plague, goddess, or mortal oppressor. To protect those who cannot protect themselves. To expose evil wearing the mask of heroism. To preserve freedom without imposing control.

**Principles:**
We reject divine mandate and mortal tyranny equally. We gather intelligence to defeat oppression before it spreads. We act with pragmatic morality, making hard choices the rigid cannot. We are pack, not hierarchy. We adapt or we fail. We question or we become what we fight.

**Promise:**
We will make mistakes. We will face impossible choices. Some of us will die. Some may fall to darkness. But we will not become tyrants to fight tyranny. We will not abandon our own. We will not serve any master but truth and justice.

- Questions own assumptions (won't follow blindly)
- Practical skills (intelligence, healing, trade, combat)
- Connected to communities (eyes and ears)
